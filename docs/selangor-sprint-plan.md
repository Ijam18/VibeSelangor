# Selangor Builder Sprint 2026
## Updated Plan (Attendance on Day 1 and Day 7 Only)

## 1. Program Structure
The sprint runs for 7 days with one live publish workshop, five troubleshooting days, and one final review day.

### Modules
| Module | Days | Objective | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|---|
| M1: Ship-First Setup and Publish | Day 1 (2 hours live) | Everyone follows instructor and publishes a working app | Live URL and GitHub repository |
| M2: Guided Build and Troubleshooting | Days 2 to 6 | Resolve blockers, improve app quality, and stabilize key flows | Daily troubleshooting log and issue fixes |
| M3: Final Review and Showcase | Day 7 | Evaluate readiness, quality, and communication of the project | Final demo and rubric score |

## 2. Session Plans

### Day 1 (120 minutes, live follow-along)
| Time | Activity | Output Check |
|---|---|---|
| 0 to 10 min | Kickoff, outcomes, and tool checklist | Accounts/tools confirmed |
| 10 to 25 min | Generate or scaffold starter app | App runs locally |
| 25 to 45 min | GitHub push workflow | Repo created and first commit done |
| 45 to 70 min | Vercel deployment demo | Production URL is live |
| 70 to 90 min | Learners follow and publish | Each learner has URL or is flagged for support |
| 90 to 110 min | Common deployment/build fixes | Top blockers resolved |
| 110 to 120 min | Exit ticket and Day 2 briefing | Progress status captured |

### Days 2 to 6 (Troubleshooting days)
| Block | Activity | Output Check |
|---|---|---|
| 0 to 15 min | Daily standup (what broke, what changed, next step) | Blocker list created |
| 15 to 75 min | Troubleshooting clinic (deploy, Git, DB, UI, API) | Issues fixed and logged |
| 75 to 90 min | Verification and commit/push | Updated URL and progress note |

### Day 7 (Review day)
| Time | Activity | Output Check |
|---|---|---|
| 0 to 20 min | Final submission freeze and setup | Final links submitted |
| 20 to 90 min | Project review presentations | Rubric scoring completed |
| 90 to 110 min | Feedback and improvement notes | Individual action points |
| 110 to 120 min | Wrap-up and next steps | Final results published |

## 3. Rubric (100 Points)
| Criterion | Weight | Excellent | Good | Needs Work |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Deployment and Reliability | 25 | Live and stable, no critical errors | Live with minor issues | Not consistently live |
| Core Functionality | 25 | Main user flow complete and working | Most flow works | Major flow broken |
| Code and Versioning | 20 | Clear structure and meaningful commits | Basic structure and commits | Disorganized history/structure |
| Troubleshooting Practice | 15 | Clear logs and effective root-cause fixes | Partial logs and fixes | Little evidence of debugging |
| Review Presentation | 15 | Clear problem-solution-impact demo | Understandable demo | Unclear or incomplete demo |

## 4. Attendance Policy (Updated)
Attendance is recorded only on Day 1 and Day 7.

### Attendance Sheet Template
| Student | Day 1 (Live Publish) | Day 7 (Review) | Attendance Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Name | P/A/L | P/A/L | Complete/Partial/At Risk | blockers/support |

Legend:
- P = Present
- A = Absent
- L = Late

Status rule:
- Complete = Present on both Day 1 and Day 7
- Partial = Present on one of the two days
- At Risk = Absent on both days

Operational notes:
- Day 1: take attendance at session start.
- Days 2 to 6: no formal attendance, only progress tracking.
- Day 7: take attendance before project reviews.
